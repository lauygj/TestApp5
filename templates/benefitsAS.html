Benefits of Active Surveillance

  • The evidence does not show a difference in the number of deaths from prostate cancer among people offered active surveillance, prostatectomy or radical radiotherapy.
  • There is some evidence that urinary function is better for people offered active surveillance than those offered prostatectomy.

      • Examples of urinary problems include:

    • feeling the need to pass water more often
    • urinary incontinence and sometimes needing a catheter(a tube in to the bladder to help you pass water)

  • There is some limited evidence that sexual function is better for people offered active surveillance than those offered prostatectomy.
  • There is some evidence that bowel function is better for people offered active surveillance than those offered radiotherapy in the short term.

      • Examples of bowel problems include:

    • increased frequency of stools
    • runny stools and passing blood
